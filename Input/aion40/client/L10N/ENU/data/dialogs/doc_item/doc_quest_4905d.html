Heintz's Story

I was barely ascended at the time. The Balaur were determined to occupy Brusthonin and sent the Zunewgishe Legion against us. We'd all heard of them and most of us were scared, but we weren't going to run.

The Red Sky Legion inspired us to stand our ground. Without the excellent leadership of Brigade General Virkel, we would have lost all of Brusthonin to the Balaur.

Ervin's Story

My perspective may be different from others you'll speak to. You see, at that time I was still a Human. We could tell that even the Daevas were afraid, and that terrified us. We couldn't escape though, so that left us no choice but to stand up to the Balaur.

Can you imagine Humans standing up to Balaur? My father made arrows for the Daevas. My mother cooked food for the soldiers.

I carried Odella powder from the village to the camp for Daevas. They'd use it to keep up their energy for fighting.

One day the whole world shook, and there was fire and then darkness. It was the Cataclysm, though I didn't know it at the time. I was scared, so I ran off to hide.

I must have been unconscious for some time, but when I woke up I knew I'd ascended. My parents died in the disaster. Considering what happened here later, I am glad they found peace.

Helmut's Story

To be honest, I'm reluctant to recall those days. Not to belittle the accomplishments of more recent battles, but Beritra's Zunewgishe Legion was the most terrifying thing I ever faced.

I was a raw recruit at the time. I shudder when I think of how incompetent I was. But if my story will help in fighting the Balaur, I'll do it.

When the Aetheric Field fell, the Balaur attacked at once. The Red Sky Legion went out to meet them.

They were the best. We all respected them tremendously. Even when Dragon Lord Beritra entered the fray, they stood firm. Thanks to their Cleric, Angeiya, they resisted the fear that Beritra projected.

They held the Balaur back long enough for the Field to come back up, but they were overwhelmed and destroyed.
