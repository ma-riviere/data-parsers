Roughing it in Ishalgen

If your feather bed has been feeling too comfy and your hide too unpunctured by insects, then Ishalgen is the rough-and-tumble adventure destination for you.

A Gastric Challenge

Sometimes the old recipes truly are the best. We hesitate to suggest that of Ungfu's cuisine, but we can safely say that innovation would not improve the fare. This restaurant is truly the finest in Ishalgen.

What's the secret ingredient in the Ishalgen Jerky? Karnif? Sparkie? Your correspondent declined to ask, and the chef himself was circumspect.

A Blessed Haze

Perhaps it's the hazy veil of sunset that makes Anturoon Crossing the most pleasant spot in Ishalgen. From this vantage, the pirate wreck Eyvindr looks almost like a picturesque ship of old, and the locals have a charmingly rustic allure.

Don't forget to pick up a souvenir postcard! The smeared-ink effect of the old-time printing technique really captures the shimmer of the sun over the water.

Get Away from it All

In the midst of the Munihele Forest nestles a cabin right out of a fairy tale. The croaking of the Ribbits' chorus makes a perfect complement to the thumping of the Daru and the tuneless humming of Verdandi, the forest's resident kindly granny. The stress and headaches are guaranteed to melt away.

Don't forget to pick up a souvenir charm, crafted in the shape of a Ribbit.
